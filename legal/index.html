Overview

How TIIO handles privacy, licensing, and user responsibilities.

TIIO is an offline-first desktop media hub from Blacnova. We do not run hosted media services; you point TIIO at files you control. These policies explain how we treat your data, what we expect from users, and how to get help if something goes wrong.

Terms of Service

Installation grants a revocable, non-transferable licence to index and play legitimately acquired media. You may install on multiple devices provided each device complies with these terms.

  • Do not redistribute the TIIO installer or updates without written permission.
  • Do not tamper with update/signature mechanisms or attempt to bypass security layers.
  • You are responsible for ensuring the media you index is lawful in your jurisdiction.
  • TIIO is beta software; features and compatibility may change without notice.

Privacy Policy

TIIO performs media indexing, playback, lyric display, and history tracking entirely on your device. We do not upload media, metadata, or usage information to Blacnova servers.

  • The application only touches the internet to check for updates or download release notes when you request it.
  • Optional diagnostics (crash reports, app version, OS version, basic GPU info) are anonymised and sent securely. No filenames, folder paths, or personal identifiers are included.
  • Support enquiries sent to tiio@blacnova.net are stored only as long as needed to close the request.

Security & Updates

Installers are signed by Blacnova and ship with published SHA-256 hashes. Verify the hash shown on the download page before installing, especially in air-gapped environments.

TIIO beta receives regular updates. Automatic updates fetch patches only from approved mirrors and can be disabled if you manage offline workstations. Policy changes will be reflected on this page alongside an effective date.

Found a security issue? Email tiio@blacnova.net with details and reproduction steps; we aim to respond within five business days.

Acceptable Use

  • Do not use TIIO to infringe copyright, violate DRM, or distribute unlicensed media.
  • Do not bundle TIIO inside third-party distributions or app stores without approval.
  • Do not probe or attack Blacnova infrastructure beyond the documented update endpoints.
